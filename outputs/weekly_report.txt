**Executive Summary**

The current state of our e-commerce operations indicates a mixed overall business health. On one hand, we have a substantial total customer base of 96,478, with 7,781 champions who are our most valued customers. However, a significant portion of our customers, 39,822, are at risk, and a considerable number, 68,837, have already churned. This suggests that while we have a solid foundation, there is a need to focus on customer retention strategies to prevent further churn and nurture our at-risk customers. Our demand forecast predicts a steady stream of 301 orders per day over the next 30 days, which is a positive indicator of ongoing business activity.

Our anomaly detection systems have flagged several key risks and alerts that require immediate attention. Notably, 149 anomalous sellers have been identified, which may indicate potential issues with product quality, pricing, or compliance. Furthermore, our worst delivery delay detected is 35.0 days, which is unacceptable and may lead to customer dissatisfaction and further churn. These anomalies pose significant risks to our customer experience, reputation, and ultimately, our revenue. It is crucial that we address these issues promptly to prevent long-term damage to our business. The high number of at-risk and churned customers also warrants a thorough review of our customer relationship management strategies to identify areas for improvement.

To mitigate these risks and capitalize on opportunities, we recommend that the operations team takes the following actions. Firstly, conduct a thorough investigation into the anomalous sellers and take corrective action to ensure compliance and quality standards are met. Secondly, review and optimize our logistics and shipping processes to reduce delivery delays and improve overall customer satisfaction. Thirdly, develop and implement targeted customer retention strategies to nurture our at-risk customers and prevent further churn. Finally, monitor our demand forecast closely and adjust our inventory management and supply chain operations accordingly to ensure we can meet the expected demand of 301 orders per day. By addressing these key areas, we can improve our overall business health, reduce risks, and drive long-term growth and success.
